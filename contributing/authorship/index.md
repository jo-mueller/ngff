# Authorship and Acknowledgement

NGFF and OME-Zarr are developed by a large community and depends on the efforts of many people.
We believe this effort should be visible and acknowledged.

The core output of the NGFF project is the specification document.
Hence, by contributing to the NGFF project, you are eligible to be listed as an author on the specification document.

We distinguish between three types of contributions:

## Tier 1: RFC contributions

This kind of contribution includes participation in the RFC process,
which is the key mechanism for the continuous development and evolution of the specification.
This kind of contribution applies to you if you did either of the following:
- Authored or co-authored an RFC
- Participated in the review process of an RFC,
  either in the form of a written (invited) review or a written comment.

For details about the RFC process, see [the RFC documentation](#rfcs).

## Tier 2: Specification contributions

Contributions to the specification document besides the RFC process are also eligible for authorship.
More specifically, these include contributions to the spec document under the [ngff-spec repository](https://github.com/ome/ngff-spec).
Such contributions include but are not limited to:
- Contributions to the schemas
- Contributions to the specification text
- Contributions to the examples
- Contributions to the tests

Authorship for this bracket is determined by commit history of the [ngff-spec repository](https://github.com/ome/ngff-spec).

## Tier 3: Community contributions

We acknowledge contributions to the greater ecosystem of ngff and ome-zarr as well, including but not limited to:
- Contributions to the ngff website (this page)
- Participation in community events (hackathons, workshops, etc.)
- Contribution to tools making use of the specification, e.g. converters, viewers, readers/writers and other consumer tools.
